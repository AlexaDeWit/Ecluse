# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

Écluse is built to be a space where people of colour — including, though by no means limited to, Black, Indigenous, and Arab communities — as well as queer people, transgender people, and disabled people, and others historically pushed to the margins of software, are safe, welcome, and **centred, not merely tolerated**. We name these communities deliberately, and never as a closed list: an unnamed "everyone" too easily defaults to white, male, and cisgender, and leaving unspoken who a space is *for* is how marginalized people get edged back out of it. Their inclusion and safety is not in tension with this project's health; it is a precondition for it, and it is not up for debate.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
* Tone-policing — dismissing, derailing, or minimizing a report of harm by criticizing the tone or manner in which it was raised rather than addressing the harm itself
* Invoking "reverse" racism, sexism, transphobia, or similar to deflect from, excuse, or draw false equivalence with harm done to marginalized people
* Demanding "neutrality," "both sides," or "civility" as a means of shielding bigotry or silencing those responding to it
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

This Code is **not neutral** between those who cause harm and those harmed by it. Where the two conflict, the safety and dignity of marginalized people take precedence over the comfort of those whose words or conduct cause harm. Complaints of "reverse" discrimination, tone-policing, or demands for false balance are not, in themselves, valid grievances under this Code.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behaviour and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behaviour that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. The **Disqualifying affiliations** provision below is an exception to this scope: it applies regardless of where the affiliation or conduct occurs.

## Disqualifying affiliations

Active and ongoing participation in, membership of, or promotion of an organized hate group — a movement whose purpose or activity is the dehumanization, persecution, or exclusion of people on the basis of the characteristics named in our Pledge (for example, neo-Nazi, white-supremacist, or anti-LGBTQ+ extremist organizations such as the Proud Boys) — is fundamentally incompatible with participation in this community.

Such affiliation is itself a credible threat to the people this Code exists to protect, regardless of how courteously the person conducts themselves within project spaces. It is grounds for **immediate and permanent removal**, at the maintainer's discretion, **without** progressing through the graduated enforcement steps below.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at [@AlexaDeWit](https://github.com/AlexaDeWit). All reports will be reviewed and investigated as fairly and carefully as the maintainer is able. Écluse currently has a single, capacity-constrained maintainer, so acknowledgement and resolution may take time; reports are taken seriously regardless of how quickly they can be addressed.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behaviour deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
